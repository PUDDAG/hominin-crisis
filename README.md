> *On January 2, 2026, satellite imagery confirmed what NGO field teams and border patrols had been reporting for 48 hours. They're back. Across multiple regions, fifteen species of extinct humans have reappeared. Disoriented, territorial, and very much alive.*

## What Is This Game?

You are the **UN Special Representative for Non-Sapiens Affairs**. Fifteen species of extinct hominins: Neanderthals, Denisovans, H. erectus, H. floresiensis, and more have mysteriously reappeared across the globe. Humanity is not ready. Neither are they.

Over 240 turns (20 in-game years), you must manage a cascading global crisis: cross-species pandemics, territorial conflicts, extremist political movements, collapsing ecosystems, and the impossible question of whether extinct humans have rights.

Every decision you make changes the trajectory of human history. There are no easy answers.

---

## How to Play

### Core Loop
1. **Enact up to 3 policies** per turn from the left panel
2. **Resolve events** as they trigger, each has 2–3 choices with real consequences
3. **End the turn** and watch the world respond
4. Repeat for 240 turns (or until something ends the game early)

### The Five Crisis Meters
| Meter | What it means | Danger zone |
|---|---|---|
| **Stability** | Global civilizational order | Below 30% = collapse spiral |
| **Disease** | Cross-species pathogen spread | 100% = game over |
| **Rights** | Non-sapiens rights movement strength | Needed for Integration win |
| **Eco Stress** | Habitat and ecosystem pressure | 50%+ starts killing species |
| **Extremism** | "Sapiens First" political movements | 75%+ = institutional seizure |

### Resources
- **Budget** : +200 each turn. Required for policies and emergency actions
- **Knowledge** : Accumulates over time. Unlocks advanced policies
- **Public Opinion** : High opinion suppresses extremism; low opinion feeds it
- **Integration** : Tracks progress toward peaceful coexistence

## Ways to Win

### 🏁 Survival Win
Reach **Turn 240** with **Stability ≥ 50%**. You managed 20 years without the world ending. Not a triumph — but a start.
*Unlocks: Neanderthal, Denisovan, Heidelbergensis, Erectus, Floresiensis species modes*

### 🌿 Integration Win (Hominini Harmony)
Reach **100% Integration Score** AND **75%+ Rights** simultaneously. The hardest win. Requires consistent pro-rights decisions across the entire game and completion of the First Contact chain.
*Unlocks: Luzonensis, Antecessor, Habilis, Ergaster, Georgicus species modes*

### 💀 Extinction Win
Let **all 15 non-sapiens species go extinct**. The planet is yours again. The game acknowledges what you did.
*Unlocks: Rudolfensis, Paranthropus, Africanus, Afarensis, Tchadensis species modes*

---

## Ways to Lose

- **Stability reaches 0%** : Civilizational collapse. Your save is **permanently wiped**. No unlocks. Start over.
- **Disease reaches 100%** : Pandemic extinction event
- **3 Extermination Orders enacted** : ICC war crimes tribunal ends your mandate
- **All Tier 1–2 species go extinct** (without the full extinction win condition) : Ecological collapse ending
- **Turn 240 with Stability below 50%** : Management failure

## Strategy Tips

### Early Game (Turns 1–30)
- **Disease is your first enemy.** Enact **Pathogen Surveillance** in your first few turns it'll costs 200 and runs passively. If Disease climbs past 30% before you have it, consider the **WHO Emergency Declaration**.
- **Budget discipline matters.** You get +200/turn. Don't blow 600 in one turn without a plan. The Vaccination Program (−80/turn ongoing) can bankrupt you if enacted too early.
- **Watch Extremism.** It starts at 20% and climbs passively. High Public Opinion is your main suppressor. The **De-Escalation Units** policy is expensive but worth it mid-game.

### Mid Game (Turns 30–120)
- **The Integration win requires both Integration Score AND Rights at 75%+.** Many players reach 100% integration but stall because they never pushed rights-affirming event choices. Read event effects carefully , `RIGHTS ↑` matters.
- **First Contact (Turn ~24)** is a guaranteed special event. Accept the Neanderthal negotiation, it unlocks the **Integration Treaty** policy, the strongest single integration push in the game.
- **Ecological Stress rises every turn.** At 50% it starts actively killing species populations. The **Wildlife Protection Order** (150 budget) is cheap and should be enacted in the first 20 turns.
- **Knowledge unlocks policies.** Safe Harbor Cities requires 300 Knowledge. Cultural Exchange requires 150. Stack knowledge-generating policies early.

### Late Game (Turns 120–240)
- **Stability below 30% is a death spiral.** Extremism accelerates when stability is low, which drops stability further. Break the loop with **UN Species Summit** or **De-Escalation Units**.
- **Species extinctions lock out win conditions.** If you're going for Integration, keep your Tier 1–2 species alive. Neanderthals and Denisovans especially as they're the keystone species for that win path.
- **The Briefing modal** at the start of each turn shows population changes and meter shifts. Read it. The delta values tell you which fires are spreading.

### Event Choices
- **Choices that cost budget for positive meter effects are almost always worth it early.** Budget recovers. Meter damage compounds.
- **Dark policies** (red border in the policy list) will come back to haunt you. Containment Zones trigger escalating protest events every turn until Rights ≥ 50% and Integration ≥ 50%. The Extermination Order triggers a war crimes tribunal after two uses.
- **Three exterminations = game over.** If you're pursuing the extinction win, use other methods (containment, disease mismanagement) to let species die naturally. Direct extermination orders are capped.

## Species Codex

All 16 species are based on **real hominin science**. Every piece of lore in the Codex is grounded in the archaeological and genetic record. Click any species dot on the map (or browse the Codex from the title screen) to read their full profile.

Species are organized by **Cognitive Tier** (1 = most complex, 5 = most primitive):
- **Tier 1** (Neanderthal, Denisovan) : Burial rituals, symbolic thought, genetic overlap with modern humans
- **Tier 2** (Heidelbergensis, Erectus, Floresiensis, Luzonensis, Antecessor) : Tool use, cooperative hunting, fire control
- **Tier 3** (Habilis, Ergaster, Georgicus, Rudolfensis) : Early Homo, stone tools, out-of-Africa dispersal
- **Tier 4** (Paranthropus) : Dietary specialists, no confirmed symbolic behavior
- **Tier 5** (Africanus, Afarensis, Tchadensis) : Australopithecines and earliest hominin candidates

## Save System

- The game **autosaves after every turn** to your browser's local storage
- Use **Export Save** to copy a text string you can paste elsewhere
- Use **Import Save** to restore an exported string
- **If Stability hits 0%**, your save is permanently deleted. This is intentional

## Map Guide

The map shows real-world regions where each species historically lived:
- **Colored dots** = species populations (size reflects population %)
- **Region fill color** = local stability (green = stable, orange = tense, red = conflict)
- **Animated trails** = migration and displacement events
- **Purple haze** = Disease Index above 40%
- **Red dashed border** = Containment Zone policy active
- **Green shield** = Safe Harbor Cities policy active
- **⚑ flag** = species you've consistently supported (helped 3+ times)

Hover over any dot to see species lore and live population data. Click a dot to trigger first contact with undetected species.
